# Prism AI India Strategy

Last updated: April 15, 2026

## Purpose

This document defines a practical India-first strategy for Prism AI based on:

- the current Prism codebase and product shape
- current Indian enterprise and regulated-market realities
- the thesis that in-app AI guidance and safe execution are valuable, but must be adapted for India rather than copied from US/Europe AI agent companies

This is a strategy document, not a pitch deck. It is written to guide product, GTM, and fundraising choices.

## Executive Summary

Prism should not position itself as a generic "AI agent in your app" company for India.

That category is now too broad and too easy to copy at the messaging layer.

Prism should instead position itself as:

**The AI workflow agent for Indian software products: multilingual, context-aware, and safe for complex onboarding, support, and operations workflows.**

The strongest near-term opportunity is not direct bank-grade automation on day one. The strongest opportunity is:

- Indian B2B SaaS with complex setup
- fintech infrastructure and software vendors serving BFSI/NBFCs
- insurance, broker, intermediary, and workflow-heavy platforms
- internal enterprise tools where training, compliance, and guided execution matter

Prism is already strong enough for pilots in these categories. It is not yet enterprise-ready for direct regulated BFSI/NBFC sales without significant hardening around compliance, privacy, approval workflows, and deployment options.

The right India strategy is:

1. sell first into high-friction software workflows
2. localize deeply for India
3. integrate Sarvam strategically for multilingual and voice layers
4. add enterprise controls and compliance posture
5. move upward into BFSI-adjacent and then regulated BFSI

## Current Product Assessment

Based on the codebase, Prism already has a credible product foundation:

- embeddable widget for in-app assistance
- backend session and flow engine
- AI action orchestration
- onboarding flow builder
- activation analytics
- failure tracking
- escalation handling
- audit logging
- knowledge base support
- integrations

This means Prism is not just a concept. It can already support:

- guided onboarding
- contextual user assistance
- basic in-product execution
- operational visibility into where users get stuck

However, for Indian enterprise and especially BFSI-style adoption, the current product should be treated as:

- pilot-ready for SaaS and fintech-adjacent companies
- not yet enterprise-ready for compliance-sensitive direct BFSI accounts

## Core Thesis for India

The problem is real, but the framing must change.

The real pain is not "companies want an AI chatbot in their app."

The real pain is:

- users abandon setup before reaching value
- support teams repeatedly answer the same blocking workflow questions
- product teams do not know exactly where users fail
- operations teams spend expensive human time pushing users through repetitive workflows
- multilingual and non-technical users struggle more in Indian software environments

Therefore the marketable Prism thesis for India is:

**Prism helps Indian software companies reduce onboarding drop-off, resolve complex in-app blockers, and guide users through critical workflows in their own language, with safe execution and enterprise controls.**

## Category Positioning

### What Prism should not be

Prism should not present itself primarily as:

- a generic AI assistant
- "ask anything" chat
- a broad customer support bot
- full autopilot for sensitive workflows
- a direct clone of Tandem

These positions are either too weak, too crowded, or too risky for India-first enterprise sales.

### What Prism should be

Prism should position itself as:

- an in-app workflow agent
- activation and onboarding infrastructure
- support deflection for complex workflow blockers
- multilingual execution and guidance layer
- enterprise-safe AI action system

A sharper positioning line:

**Prism is the in-app AI workflow agent for Indian software products. It sees user context, explains what to do, guides the next step, and safely completes repeatable actions inside the product.**

India-specific positioning line:

**Prism helps users complete complex product workflows in English, Hindi, Hinglish, and other Indian languages, with safe execution, auditability, and enterprise controls.**

## Why India Is Attractive

India is attractive because many software workflows are:

- high-friction
- multi-step
- compliance-sensitive
- multilingual
- under-supported by human teams

This is especially true in:

- fintech
- insurance
- lending operations
- collections
- HR and payroll
- GST and accounting software
- internal operations software
- enterprise training and process software

India also creates a strong wedge for a localized product because:

- language matters more
- voice matters more
- guided workflows matter more for less technical users
- enterprise buyers increasingly care about local deployment and sovereign AI narratives

## Tandem-Inspired, But Not Tandem-Copied

Tandem validates that there is demand for an embedded AI agent layer inside software products.

Useful lessons from that category:

- in-product context matters
- execution matters more than document search alone
- activation and support ROI are strong buying angles
- snippet-based deployment lowers friction
- product-led configuration is more scalable than engineering-heavy implementation

But Prism should not copy Tandem's exact pitch because that pitch is:

- US/Europe-first
- PLG-heavy
- generic at the category level
- not localized for India
- not optimized for Indian enterprise buying behavior

Prism should build an India-native version of the category:

**workflow AI + multilingual UX + safe execution + enterprise controls + vertical templates**

## Strategic Use of Sarvam AI

Sarvam should not replace Prism's reasoning and workflow orchestration layer. Sarvam should strengthen Prism's India-first moat.

### Recommended role of Sarvam in the Prism stack

Use Sarvam primarily for:

- speech-to-text for Indian languages
- text-to-speech for Indian languages
- translation and transliteration
- document intelligence for forms and document-heavy workflows
- sovereign / India-operated AI positioning for enterprise buyers

### Recommended product architecture

Prism should use a layered architecture:

1. **Workflow orchestration layer**
   - Prism backend
   - step engine
   - session engine
   - action policy engine
   - audit and approvals

2. **Best-model reasoning layer**
   - use the strongest model for complex reasoning, tool selection, and guardrails
   - this layer can remain model-flexible

3. **India language layer**
   - Sarvam for STT, TTS, translation, and multilingual interfaces

4. **Execution layer**
   - Prism widget
   - guided actions
   - safe page actions
   - selector healing

5. **Compliance and deployment layer**
   - masking
   - approvals
   - audit trails
   - region and deployment controls

### Why Sarvam is strategically useful

Sarvam gives Prism a stronger India story in:

- multilingual support
- voice-based assistance
- regional language accessibility
- enterprise comfort with India-operated AI infrastructure narratives
- regulated-industry deployment discussions

This is more defensible in India than a pure "generic AI copilot" positioning.

## Ideal Customer Profile

Prism is not for all SaaS.

The ideal customer profile is:

- software product with complex multi-step workflows
- measurable activation or onboarding drop-off
- expensive support or CS intervention during setup
- recurring process questions inside the app
- willingness to instrument and improve workflow completion

### Best-fit characteristics

Best-fit companies have at least 3 of these:

- onboarding requires multiple steps
- activation rate is weak or inconsistent
- support gets many "how do I do this?" tickets
- success teams manually walk users through setup
- users are not deeply technical
- workflows vary by role, plan, or account configuration
- users may prefer local language explanations

### Near-term ICP priority list

#### Tier 1: strongest starting wedge

- fintech infrastructure software
- lending operations software vendors
- insurance distribution and intermediary software
- HR/payroll/attendance/compliance products
- GST/accounting/procurement software
- workflow-heavy B2B SaaS with setup friction

#### Tier 2: strong expansion wedge

- products selling into NBFCs
- products selling into insurers
- internal enterprise operations and training tools
- field-force and collections software
- B2B platforms with high support volume during setup

#### Tier 3: later, after hardening

- direct NBFC sales
- direct regulated BFSI deployments
- highly sensitive KYC and lending journeys
- high-trust enterprise workflow automation

## Product Strategy for India

### Product thesis

Prism should be built around 4 modes:

1. **Explain**
   - answer workflow questions in plain language
   - explain current screen and next step
   - provide role-aware and context-aware guidance

2. **Guide**
   - highlight fields and actions
   - provide step-by-step assistance
   - reduce confusion inside complex flows

3. **Assist**
   - prefill repeatable fields
   - navigate safely
   - execute low-risk actions
   - remain under action policies

4. **Escalate**
   - hand off to human support or ops with context
   - preserve conversation and workflow state

This is stronger and safer than a blanket autopilot promise.

### India-specific product additions

Prism should add the following India-first features:

#### Multilingual UX

- Hindi support
- Hinglish understanding
- regional language roadmap
- mixed-language input handling
- translated UI responses
- translated step instructions

#### Voice layer

- voice input for workflow questions
- text-to-speech for spoken guidance
- voice-first mode for less technical users

#### India-specific workflow templates

- payroll setup
- GST onboarding
- lending ops setup
- collections workflow setup
- insurance intermediary onboarding
- broker and advisor workflow onboarding

#### Safe-execution controls

- recommend-only mode
- approve-before-execute mode
- full execution only on allowlisted actions
- field sensitivity controls
- no-autonomous-submit mode

#### Admin and ops controls

- replay of what happened in a session
- evidence trail per action
- configurable escalation rules
- workflow success/failure drilldowns
- segment-wise drop-off analytics

## Enterprise Hardening Required Before BFSI Sales

This is the most important gap area.

Before Prism sells seriously into Indian regulated sectors, it needs:

### Identity and access

- SSO
- SAML
- granular RBAC
- audit-friendly admin roles

### Privacy and data controls

- masking and redaction of PII in logs and prompts
- configurable retention
- deletion workflows
- consent capture and consent logging
- data minimization controls

### Security and deployment

- VPC deployment option
- on-prem or private-hosted option for some buyers
- encryption key management
- secrets management posture
- security documentation pack

### Compliance and safety

- immutable or exportable audit logs
- human approval workflow for sensitive actions
- page, selector, and action allowlists
- blocklists for sensitive fields
- policy engine for what the agent can do

### Procurement readiness

- architecture diagram
- DPA
- security whitepaper
- incident response summary
- standard questionnaire responses

Without these, direct BFSI/NBFC enterprise sales will be much harder.

## Messaging Strategy

### Primary message

**Prism helps users complete complex workflows inside your product, in their own language, with AI guidance, safe execution, and enterprise controls.**

### Secondary message

**Reduce onboarding drop-off, support load, and workflow confusion without adding headcount.**

### Messaging pillars

#### 1. Workflow completion

Users do not need another chatbot. They need help finishing the task they are stuck on.

#### 2. Product context

Prism understands what page the user is on, what step they are in, and what actions are possible.

#### 3. Safe execution

Prism can explain, guide, or act depending on policy, workflow type, and customer trust level.

#### 4. India-native UX

Prism supports multilingual and voice-forward assistance for Indian users and operations teams.

#### 5. Enterprise trust

Prism is built for auditability, escalation, and controlled execution, not black-box automation.

### Messaging to avoid

Avoid leading with:

- "ask anything"
- "AI for everything"
- "autonomous agent that does everything"
- "set up in minutes" as the only value story

These are too broad and weaken enterprise credibility.

## Sales Strategy

### Founder-led sales motion

The first sales motion should focus on:

- activation pain
- support burden
- workflow complexity
- multilingual user friction

Do not lead with technology novelty.

Lead with business pain:

- where users drop off
- what support spends time on
- how long activation takes
- where implementation gets stuck

### Sales questions for discovery

Ask prospects:

1. Which workflows cause the most user confusion?
2. Where do users drop off before reaching value?
3. How much support or CS time is spent pushing users through setup?
4. Which tasks are repeated every week by support or ops?
5. Do users struggle more because of product complexity, poor docs, or language?
6. Would your team allow guided actions, approval-based actions, or full actioning?
7. What compliance or security concerns would block rollout?

### Pilot offer

Initial pilots should focus on 1 or 2 workflows only:

- one onboarding journey
- one support-heavy workflow

Pilot success should be measured by:

- activation lift
- support ticket reduction
- faster completion time
- fewer stuck sessions

## Strategic Risks

### Risk 1: becoming a generic copilot

If Prism stays generic, it will be hard to stand out.

Mitigation:

- focus on workflow completion
- focus on India-specific strengths
- focus on vertical templates

### Risk 2: overpromising autonomy

If Prism markets itself as full autopilot too early, enterprise trust will fall.

Mitigation:

- lead with guided and safe execution
- make autonomy policy-driven

### Risk 3: trying to sell directly to BFSI too early

Sales cycles will be long and requirements heavy.

Mitigation:

- start with BFSI-adjacent vendors and workflow-heavy SaaS

### Risk 4: weak localization

If Prism says "India-first" but only supports English, the wedge is weak.

Mitigation:

- implement multilingual and voice capabilities early

## Bottom-Line Recommendation

Prism should pursue an India-first strategy, but not as a broad AI assistant company.

The strongest strategy is:

**Build Prism as the multilingual in-app workflow agent for Indian software products, starting with onboarding and support-heavy workflows in SaaS, fintech infrastructure, and BFSI-adjacent software, then harden for regulated enterprise sales.**

That gives Prism a sharper story than:

- generic copilot
- generic support bot
- generic AI agent platform

It also makes Sarvam integration strategically useful rather than cosmetic.

## Final Positioning Statement

**Prism is the in-app AI workflow agent for Indian software products. It helps users complete complex onboarding, support, and operations tasks in their own language, with context-aware guidance, safe execution, and enterprise controls.**
